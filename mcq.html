📐 1. Introduction to Basic Geodesy
1Who is credited with one of the earliest scientific estimates of Earth's circumference?
A) Ptolemy
B) Eratosthenes
C) Isaac Newton
D) Galileo Galilei
Answer: B
Eratosthenes estimated Earth's circumference around 240 BCE by measuring shadows at two different locations (Alexandria and Syene).
2The geoid represents the exact mathematical shape of the Earth.
A) True
B) False
Answer: B (False)
The geoid is an equipotential surface of Earth's gravity field, approximating mean sea level. It is irregular and lumpy, not a perfect mathematical shape. The ellipsoid is the mathematical model.
3The ________ is the theoretical surface that represents mean sea level extended continuously through the continents.
Answer: Geoid
The geoid is the equipotential surface of the Earth's gravity field that best approximates mean sea level. It serves as the reference surface for orthometric heights.
4Which of the following best describes the shape of the Earth?
A) Perfect sphere
B) Oblate spheroid (flattened at poles, bulging at equator)
C) Prolate spheroid (elongated at poles)
D) Perfect ellipsoid
Answer: B
Earth is an oblate spheroid — it is flattened at the poles and bulges at the equator due to rotational forces. This is why reference ellipsoids are used to model Earth's shape.
5The reference ellipsoid is a simplified mathematical model of the Earth's shape used for geodetic calculations.
A) True
B) False
Answer: A (True)
The reference ellipsoid is indeed a mathematical model (a flattened sphere) used to define geographic coordinates and perform geodetic calculations. Different datums use different ellipsoids.
6The difference in elevation between the geoid and the reference ellipsoid at any given point is called the ________.
Answer: Geoid undulation (or geoid height / geoid separation)
Geoid undulation represents the vertical distance between the geoid (mean sea level surface) and the mathematical ellipsoid at a specific point. This value varies across the globe.
7Which scientist first proposed that Earth is an oblate spheroid based on gravitational theory?
A) Albert Einstein
B) Isaac Newton
C) Carl Friedrich Gauss
D) Jean-Baptiste Joseph Delambre
Answer: B
Isaac Newton, in his Principia Mathematica (1687), theoretically predicted that Earth would be an oblate spheroid due to its rotation. This was later confirmed by geodetic surveys.
8The geoid is perfectly smooth and uniform across the entire Earth.
A) True
B) False
Answer: B (False)
The geoid is irregular and lumpy due to variations in Earth's gravity caused by uneven mass distribution (mountains, ocean trenches, mantle density variations). It deviates from the smooth ellipsoid by up to ±100 meters.
🗺️ 2. Introduction to Coordinate Systems
9In the geographic coordinate system, latitude measures angular distance:
A) East or West from the Prime Meridian
B) North or South from the Equator
C) From the center of the Earth
D) Along the surface of the geoid
Answer: B
Latitude measures angular distance North or South from the Equator (0°), ranging from 90°N (North Pole) to 90°S (South Pole). Longitude measures East-West from the Prime Meridian.
10In the ECEF (Earth-Centered Earth-Fixed) coordinate system, the Z-axis points toward the ________.
Answer: North Pole (or Conventional Terrestrial Pole / CTP)
In ECEF coordinates, the Z-axis aligns with Earth's rotational axis pointing toward the North Pole. The X-axis points toward the intersection of the Equator and Prime Meridian (0°E, 0°N), and Y completes the right-handed system.
11The UTM coordinate system divides the world into how many zones?
A) 36 zones
B) 60 zones
C) 180 zones
D) 360 zones
Answer: B
UTM divides the world into 60 zones, each 6° of longitude wide, numbered 1 to 60 starting from 180°W. Polar regions (above 84°N and below 80°S) use the Universal Polar Stereographic (UPS) system instead.
12In UTM coordinates, the false easting value of 500,000 meters is added to avoid negative coordinates.
A) True
B) False
Answer: A (True)
Each UTM zone has a central meridian assigned a false easting of 500,000 meters. This ensures all easting values within a zone are positive numbers, making calculations and data management easier.
13Which coordinate system is specifically designed for the polar regions?
A) UTM
B) State Plane Coordinate System
C) Universal Polar Stereographic (UPS)
D) GEOREF
Answer: C
UPS covers polar regions (north of 84°N and south of 80°S) where UTM projections become too distorted. It uses a polar stereographic projection with the pole as the origin.
14The ________ coordinate system uses a 4-character alphanumeric code to identify 15° × 15° quadrangles worldwide.
Answer: GEOREF (World Geographic Reference System)
GEOREF divides the world into 15° × 15° quadrangles identified by two letters, then subdivides into smaller units. It was developed for military and aviation use for rapid position reporting.
15Which of the following is a primary purpose of the State Plane Coordinate System (SPCS) in the United States?
A) To provide a single global coordinate system
B) To minimize distortion for large-scale mapping within individual states
C) To replace the GPS coordinate system
D) To map only federal lands
Answer: B
SPCS was designed to provide a high-accuracy coordinate system for each U.S. state with minimal distortion. Each state has one or more zones using either Transverse Mercator or Lambert Conformal Conic projections.
16The Maidenhead Locator System (Grid Squares) is primarily used by amateur radio operators to specify locations.
A) True
B) False
Answer: A (True)
The Maidenhead Locator System uses a combination of letters and numbers (e.g., "FM18lw") to encode latitude and longitude in a compact format. It is widely used in amateur radio (ham radio) for location reporting.
17In navigation systems, WGS 84 is best described as:
A) A type of map projection
B) A global geodetic datum used by GPS
C) A local coordinate system for Europe
D) A postal code system
Answer: B
WGS 84 (World Geodetic System 1984) is the global datum used by GPS. It defines a reference ellipsoid, coordinate system, and gravity model, providing a consistent framework for global positioning.
18In the Northern Hemisphere UTM system, northing values are measured from the ________.
Answer: Equator (with a value of 0 meters)
In the Northern Hemisphere, northing starts at 0 at the Equator and increases northward. In the Southern Hemisphere, a false northing of 10,000,000 meters is applied at the Equator to keep all values positive.
19Which of the following is NOT a global coordinate system?
A) WGS 84
B) ITRF (International Terrestrial Reference Frame)
C) State Plane Coordinate System
D) ECEF (Earth-Centered Earth-Fixed)
Answer: C
The State Plane Coordinate System is a local (U.S.-specific) system designed for individual states. WGS 84, ITRF, and ECEF are all global systems used for worldwide positioning and navigation.
20Longitude values range from 0° to 180° East and 0° to 180° West.
A) True
B) False
Answer: A (True)
Longitude measures angular distance East or West from the Prime Meridian (0°), ranging to 180°E and 180°W. The 180° meridian is approximately the International Date Line.
🎯 3. Projection Systems
21Which class of projections is best suited for mapping areas with a predominantly east-west extent?
A) Cylindrical projections
B) Conic projections
C) Azimuthal projections
D) Pseudocylindrical projections
Answer: B
Conic projections are ideal for mid-latitude regions with east-west extent (e.g., the United States, Russia). The cone is placed over the region, minimizing distortion along standard parallels.
22A conformal projection preserves the shape of small areas but distorts area sizes.
A) True
B) False
Answer: A (True)
Conformal (orthomorphic) projections preserve local shapes and angles, making them suitable for navigation and topographic maps. However, they distort area, especially at high latitudes (e.g., Greenland appears oversized on Mercator).
23A projection that preserves area relationships is called an ________ projection.
Answer: Equal-area (or equivalent / authalic)
Equal-area projections maintain correct area ratios, making them suitable for thematic maps showing population density, land use, or other areal distributions. Examples include Albers Equal-Area Conic and Mollweide.
24The Mercator projection is an example of which projection type?
A) Conic projection
B) Azimuthal projection
C) Cylindrical projection
D) Pseudoconic projection
Answer: C
Mercator is a cylindrical projection where the globe is projected onto a cylinder wrapped around the Equator. It is conformal (preserves angles) and widely used for navigation because rhumb lines (constant bearing) appear as straight lines.
25Which projection property is most important for a map used in aviation route planning?
A) Equal area
B) Conformality (preservation of angles/shapes)
C) Equidistance (true distances from center)
D) Compromise (balanced distortion)
Answer: B
Conformality is crucial for aviation because it preserves angles, allowing pilots to plot straight-line courses (rhumb lines) with constant compass bearings. The Mercator projection is historically used for this purpose.
26Azimuthal projections are best suited for mapping polar regions.
A) True
B) False
Answer: A (True)
Azimuthal projections project the globe onto a flat plane tangent at a point (often a pole). They excel at showing polar regions with minimal distortion near the center point, making them ideal for Arctic and Antarctic maps.
27The ________ projection is a cylindrical projection that shows the entire world but significantly distorts areas near the poles.
Answer: Mercator
The Mercator projection preserves angles and shapes locally but greatly exaggerates areas at high latitudes. Greenland appears larger than it actually is relative to equatorial regions. This has led to criticism for its cultural implications.
28Which of the following is a characteristic of the Transverse Mercator projection used in UTM?
A) The cylinder is tangent to the Equator
B) The cylinder is rotated 90° and tangent to a meridian
C) The projection surface is a cone
D) The projection surface is a flat plane
Answer: B
In Transverse Mercator, the cylinder is rotated 90° (transverse) so it is tangent to a meridian (central meridian of each UTM zone) rather than the Equator. This minimizes distortion in north-south oriented zones.
29No map projection can simultaneously preserve both area and shape perfectly for the entire Earth.
A) True
B) False
Answer: A (True)
This is a fundamental theorem of cartography (related to Gauss's Theorema Egregium). It is impossible to flatten a sphere onto a plane without distortion. Projections must compromise — preserving some properties while distorting others.
30A map that has not been projected and simply uses latitude and longitude as x-y coordinates is called an ________ map.
Answer: Unprojected (or geographic / plate carrée)
An unprojected map treats longitude as x and latitude as y in a simple Cartesian grid. While easy to create, it causes significant distortion in shape and area, especially at higher latitudes where meridians converge.
📍 4. Geodetic Datums
31A geodetic datum primarily defines:
A) The color scheme of a map
B) The origin and orientation of a coordinate system relative to Earth
C) The scale of a map
D) The legend symbols used
Answer: B
A datum defines the position and orientation of the reference ellipsoid relative to Earth's center and surface. It provides the framework for measuring precise locations, consisting of an ellipsoid and an origin point.
32Datum shifts occur because different datums use different reference ellipsoids and origin points.
A) True
B) False
Answer: A (True)
When converting between datums (e.g., NAD27 to NAD83), coordinates shift because the underlying ellipsoids have different sizes, shapes, and positions relative to Earth's center of mass. Shifts can range from meters to hundreds of meters.
33The process of converting coordinates from one datum to another is called ________.
Answer: Datum transformation (or datum conversion)
Datum transformations use mathematical models (e.g., Helmert transformation, Molodensky transformation, or grid-based methods like NADCON) to convert coordinates between different datums. This is essential when combining data from different sources.
34Which of the following pairs represents an older datum and its newer replacement in the United States?
A) WGS 84 and ITRF 2014
B) NAD27 and NAD83
C) ED50 and ETRS89
D) Tokyo Datum and JGD2000
Answer: B
NAD27 (North American Datum 1927) was based on the Clarke 1866 ellipsoid with a ground-based origin. NAD83 (1983) is satellite-based, using the GRS 80 ellipsoid with an Earth-centered origin. Shifts between them can exceed 100 meters.
35A geocentric datum differs from a local datum primarily in that:
A) It uses a different map projection
B) Its origin is at Earth's center of mass rather than a surface point
C) It only works for small countries
D) It does not use an ellipsoid
Answer: B
Geocentric datums (like WGS 84, NAD83) have their origin at Earth's center of mass, making them globally consistent. Local datums (like NAD27, Tokyo Datum) have origins tied to specific regional survey points, optimized for local accuracy.
36Modern GPS receivers automatically use WGS 84 as their reference datum.
A) True
B) False
Answer: A (True)
GPS satellites broadcast their positions in WGS 84. Most GPS receivers natively compute positions in WGS 84 and can optionally transform to local datums (e.g., NAD83, ETRS89) for display or mapping purposes.
37The ________ is the physical surface of the Earth including all terrain features like mountains and valleys.
Answer: Topographic surface (or physical surface / Earth's surface)
The topographic surface is the actual physical surface we live on. It differs from the geoid (mean sea level surface) and the ellipsoid (mathematical model). Heights above the geoid are called orthometric heights.
38Which statement about datum conversions is correct?
A) Datum conversions are always exact with no errors
B) Datum conversions may introduce positional errors of several meters
C) Datum conversions are only needed for paper maps
D) Datum conversions change the map projection type
Answer: B
Datum transformations are approximations. Depending on the method used (simple 3-parameter vs. grid-based 7-parameter), errors can range from centimeters to several meters. High-precision applications require careful transformation method selection.
39ITRF (International Terrestrial Reference Frame) is updated periodically to account for tectonic plate motion.
A) True
B) False
Answer: A (True)
ITRF is updated every few years (e.g., ITRF2014, ITRF2020) because tectonic plates move several centimeters per year. These updates ensure the reference frame remains accurate for high-precision geodetic applications and scientific research.
40A ________ datum provides the reference for measuring elevations (heights) above mean sea level.
Answer: Vertical datum
A vertical datum defines the zero elevation surface (typically the geoid / mean sea level) for measuring heights. Examples include NAVD88 (North American Vertical Datum 1988) and EGM96 (Earth Gravitational Model 1996).
🗺️ 5. Map Elements & Types
41Which of the following is NOT considered an essential map element?
A) Title
B) Scale indicator
C) North arrow
D) Price of the map
Answer: D
Essential map elements include: title, legend, scale bar, north arrow, coordinate grid, projection information, data source/credits, and date. The price is a commercial detail, not a cartographic element.
42A thematic map focuses on showing the spatial distribution of a specific theme or topic.
A) True
B) False
Answer: A (True)
Thematic maps display specific themes such as population density, climate zones, land use, or election results. They differ from reference maps (topographic maps, road maps) which show general geographic features.
43A map that shows elevation using contour lines is called a ________ map.
Answer: Topographic
Topographic maps use contour lines to represent elevation and terrain relief. Each contour line connects points of equal elevation, allowing users to visualize hills, valleys, and slopes. Examples include USGS topographic quadrangle maps.
44Which map type would be most appropriate for showing population density across a country?
A) Road map
B) Choropleth map
C) Nautical chart
D) Aeronautical chart
Answer: B
A choropleth map uses color shading or patterns to represent data values (like population density) within predefined areas (counties, states, census tracts). Darker shades typically indicate higher values.
45The statement "1 inch equals 1 mile" on a map represents which type of scale?
A) Representative fraction (RF)
B) Verbal scale
C) Graphic scale (bar scale)
D) Digital scale
Answer: B
A verbal scale uses words to describe the relationship (e.g., "1 inch to 1 mile"). RF uses a ratio (1:63,360). A graphic scale is a visual bar that remains accurate when the map is enlarged or reduced.
46A cadastral map shows property boundaries and land ownership information.
A) True
B) False
Answer: A (True)
Cadastral maps (or parcel maps) show property boundaries, ownership, and land registration information. They are essential for land administration, taxation, and legal land transactions.
47The ________ on a map explains what the symbols, colors, and patterns represent.
Answer: Legend (or map key)
The legend is a crucial map element that decodes the symbology used on the map. It explains what each color, line type, symbol, and pattern represents, enabling users to correctly interpret the map's information.
48Which of the following map types is specifically designed for marine navigation?
A) Topographic map
B) Nautical chart
C) Choropleth map
D) Geological map
Answer: B
Nautical charts are designed for marine navigation, showing water depths, shoreline features, navigational aids (buoys, lighthouses), hazards, and tidal information. They use specialized symbology and are produced by hydrographic offices.
49On small-scale maps, a large area is shown with less detail.
A) True
B) False
Answer: A (True)
Scale is a ratio: small-scale maps have a small ratio (e.g., 1:1,000,000) showing large areas (continents, world) with generalized detail. Large-scale maps (e.g., 1:1,000) show small areas with high detail (city blocks, building footprints).
50A map that uses dots to represent quantities or occurrences at specific locations is called a ________ map.
Answer: Dot distribution (or dot density)
Dot distribution maps use dots to represent the presence, quantity, or frequency of a phenomenon. Each dot may represent one unit or a specified number of units (e.g., one dot = 1,000 people). They are useful for showing spatial patterns.
